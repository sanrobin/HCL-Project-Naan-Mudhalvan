About the Project

Addressing Cloud Fund's fraud prevention challenges through human-centered design

The Challenge

Cloud Fund's fraud prevention efforts face challenges beyond technology: disconnected workflows, siloed systems, and reactive approaches that create friction for legitimate users while still leaving vulnerabilities for bad actors.

Despite investments in fraud prevention technologies, the company continues to struggle with effectively detecting, mitigating, and responding to increasingly sophisticated fraudulent activities in a timely manner.

Our Approach

We applied design thinking to unearth meaningful behavioral, operational, and systemic insights to reshape fraud defense strategies and systems.

By placing humans at the center of our process, we created solutions that are not only more effective at preventing fraud but also enhance the user experience and build trust with customers and stakeholders.

Project Objectives

  • Discover true user pain points across the fraud prevention journey
  • Identify internal process breakdowns and communication gaps
  • Design intuitive, agile solutions that scale with new threats
  • Create a cohesive system that balances security with usability
  • Develop adaptive fraud detection mechanisms using AI and machine learning
  • Establish continuous feedback loops for ongoing improvement

Design Thinking Process

Our methodology focused on understanding the human elements of fraud prevention before jumping to technical solutions.

Design thinking provided a framework to address Cloud Fund's complex fraud prevention challenges by focusing on human needs, behaviors, and experiences. This approach helped us bridge the gap between security requirements and user experience.

Empathize

We conducted field interviews with users, fraud victims, and support teams to understand the emotional and practical impacts of both fraud incidents and prevention measures.

Activities:

  • User interviews with customers who experienced fraud
  • Shadowing fraud analysts during investigations
  • Customer journey mapping across touchpoints
  • Stakeholder interviews across departments

Tools Used:

Empathy Maps Contextual Inquiry User Shadowing

Define

We synthesized our research into journey maps and problem statements that highlighted the key pain points and opportunities for improvement.

Outputs:

  • User personas for customers and fraud analysts
  • Journey maps highlighting friction points
  • Problem statements focused on security-usability balance
  • Opportunity areas for innovation

Tools Used:

Affinity Diagrams Journey Mapping Problem Framing

Insights Uncovered

Our design thinking approach revealed critical insights that traditional analysis might have missed.

Misaligned KPIs

Fraud operations teams were measured on fraud prevention rates, while UX teams were measured on conversion rates—creating an inherent conflict in goals and approaches.

Verification Abandonment

Users were abandoning identity verification processes due to confusing flows and lack of context about why additional security was needed.

Alert Fatigue

Analysts were overwhelmed by siloed alerts across multiple platforms, leading to missed signals and delayed responses to potential fraud.

Trust Signals

Users were more willing to complete security steps when they understood the purpose and saw visual confirmation of security measures.
